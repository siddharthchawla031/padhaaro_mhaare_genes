Symposium Speakers

Distinguished researchers presenting at Padhaaro Mhaare Genes

Ashwin Dalal

CDFD

Medical Genetics

Mission program in pediatric rare genetic disorders. The DBT-funded program for pediatric rare genetic disorders spans 16 clinical centres nationwide, offering diagnostics, research, and counselling through telemedicine and genome sequencing. Novel disease gene variants are functionally validated using cell lines, Drosophila, zebrafish, and mouse models, and bridge research and clinical applications.

Pankaj Yadav

IIT-J

Single-cell & Spatial Omics

Annotating cell types and sub-types in single-cell and spatial omics. Cell-type annotations rely on single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) references or predefined marker sets. I will present ScInfeR, a graph-based cell-type annotation method that combines information from scRNA-seq references and marker sets. Benchmarking across atlas-scale scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq, and spatial datasets, evaluating 10 existing tools in over 100 cell-type prediction tasks, demonstrates ScInfeR’s superior performance.

Shailendra Goel

University of Delhi

Plant Genetics & Genomics

Genomics empowers plant genetics. New genomics tools empower studies on crops like Carthamus tinctorius and environmentally important keystone species like Primula, allowing insights into genes involved in adaptation in natural environments.

Divya Tej Sowpati

CCMB

Human Genomics & Epigenomics

Structural Variants in Indian Populations. Large genetic variants contribute to over 20 Mb of variation per genome. Using long-read sequencing technologies, we uncover the structural variant landscape from diverse healthy Indian samples.

Abhijit A. Sardesai

CDFD

Bacterial Genetics & Physiology

A phosphorelay and a silent potassium transporter. Under laboratory conditions, some microbial solute transport processes are naturally rendered silent. I will discuss the silencing of the potassium efflux protein YcgO by the unphosphorylated state of PtsN in E. coli.

Nishant K.T

IISER-Tvm

Yeast Genetics, DNA Recombination & Repair

Regulation of Loss of Heterozygosity by Environmental Factors. Chronic blue light exposure causes extensive LOH through ROS production, showing how ubiquitous environmental factors regulate LOH frequency and distribution through distinct mechanisms.

Bhaswati Pandit

NIMG

Infectious Diseases & Immunology (TB)

Genetic underpinning of host factors in tuberculosis. Host genetic factors play a major role in determining susceptibility to infection. We carried out genetic studies on patients with TB and their healthy household contacts to identify such factors.

Anshu Bharadwaj

IMTECH

Genome Analysis & Data Science

AI and structure driven approach for genome annotation. Applications of 3Di alphabet in bacterial genome annotation and data-driven approaches in infection biology, focusing on antimicrobial resistance.

Himanshu Sinha

IIT-Madras

Quantitative Genetics, Genomics, Transcriptomics

Activating the hidden backup circuit: Flip both switches simultaneously to change system behaviour. Two yeast variants unlock a latent arginine biosynthesis pathway, revealing how variant combinations rewire metabolism beyond additive effects.

Jahnavi Joshi

IISER-Pune

Evolutionary & Ecological Biology

Evolutionary and ecological perspectives on venom evolution in predatory soil arthropods. Using proteo-transcriptomics, we study venom evolution in scolopendrid centipedes. Venom proteins, particularly scoloptoxins, show strong positive selection, and venom diversity correlates with body size and ecological factors like diet.

Aravind N A

IISER-Trivandrum

Freshwater Biodiversity & Evolution

Clade age drove freshwater gastropod diversification. Older clades of freshwater snails and slugs in the Western Ghats are more species-rich. Miocene sea-level fluctuations and monsoon patterns caused a decline in diversification, while shifts to freshwater habitats and direct development did not significantly impact speciation rates.

Shankar Manoharan

CCMB

Microbial Pathogenesis

Armour less but not defenceless: Klebsiella pneumoniae adaptation to the neonatal bloodstream. Capsule-inactivating insertions in neonatal sepsis isolates reveal adaptation mechanisms, showing how secondary mutations compensate for capsule loss and enable bloodstream survival.

Sucharita Dey

IISc-Bangalore

Protein Engineering & Evolution

Identifying the molecular determinants of proteins’ robustness to mutations. Structural compactness, local atomic density, and hydrogen bonds influence protein evolvability. Disease-causing vs neutral mutations are used in a machine learning classifier to predict deleterious substitution effects.

Sushmita Jha

NCCS

Neuro-oncology & Immunology

Unlocking Glioma Mysteries: Biomarkers, patient-derived organoids, and tumour-immune dialogues. Biomarker discovery and organoids reveal tumour diversity, microenvironment interactions, and actionable pathways for targeted drug testing.

Murali Dharan Bashyam

IISc-Bangalore

Cancer Genomics & Chromatin Biology

‘I open at the close’: Gene fusions activate genes from closed chromatin compartments across cancer types. Fusion of genes from open and closed compartments activates the 3’ (B) partner gene, driven by transcriptional signals of the 5’ (A) partner gene.

Madhusudan Reddy Nandineni

CCMB

Forensic Genetics

DNA profiling in criminal investigations and mass disaster victim identification. Highlights landmark cases, methodologies, and challenges in DNA collection and analysis, showing the importance of genetic evidence in solving crimes and providing closure to families.
