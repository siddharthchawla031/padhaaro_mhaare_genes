Symposium Speakers

Distinguished researchers presenting at Padhaaro Mhaare Genes

Ashwin Dalal

CDFD

Medical Genetics

Mission program in pediatric rare genetic disorders. The DBT-funded program for pediatric rare genetic disorders spans 16 clinical centres nationwide, offering diagnostics, research, and counselling through telemedicine and genome sequencing. Novel disease gene variants are functionally validated using cell lines, Drosophila, zebrafish and mouse models, and to bridge research and clinical applications.

Pankaj Yadav

IIT-J

Single-cell & Spatial Omics

Annotating cell types and sub-types in single-cell and spatial omics. Cell-type annotations rely on single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) references or predefined marker sets. However, limited high-quality references and marker sets makes this prone to bias and limits usability. I will present ScInfeR, a graph-based cell-type annotation method that combines information from scRNA-seq references and marker sets. Benchmarking across atlas-scale scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq, and spatial datasets, evaluating 10 existing tools in over 100 cell-type prediction tasks, has demonstrated ScInfeR’s superior performance.

Shailendra Goel

University of Delhi

Plant Genetics & Genomics

Genomics empowers plant genetics. My talk will explore how new genomics tools have empowered studies not only on crops like Carthamus tinctorius but also environmentally important keystone species like Primula. It is now possible to look at genes involved in adaptation in natural environments.

Divya Tej Sowpati

CCMB

Human Genomics & Epigenomics

Structural Variants in Indian Populations. Large genetic variants called structural variants contribute to over 20 Mb of variation per genome. Despite their prominence, structural variants are often overlooked in large-scale studies due to technical challenges. I will discuss our efforts in utilizing new long-read sequencing technologies to uncover the structural variant landscape from diverse healthy Indian samples.

Abhijit A. Sardesai

CDFD

Bacterial Genetics & Physiology

A phosphorelay and a silent potassium transporter. Under laboratory growth conditions, some microbial solute transport processes are naturally rendered silent. Transporter silencing may be because although these transporters may allow for fitness in certain environments, in a neutral environment silencing prevents wasteful transport cycles. I will discuss the silencing of a potassium efflux protein YcgO by the unphosphorylated state of PtsN, the terminal phospho-acceptor protein of the PtsP-PtsO-PtsN phosphorelay, in E. coli.

Nishant K.T

IISER-Tvm

Yeast Genetics, DNA Recombination & Repair

Regulation of Loss of Heterozygosity by Environmental Factors. Loss of Heterozygosity (LOH) is frequently associated with the development of various cancers. We show that chronic blue light exposure causes extensive LOH through the production of reactive oxygen species and has a unique mutation signature comprising long LOH tracts, small indels and transversion mutations. Our results suggest ubiquitous environmental factors regulate LOH frequency and distribution through distinct mechanisms.

Bhaswati Pandit

NIMG

Infectious Diseases & Immunology (TB)

Genetic underpinning of host factors in tuberculosis. Traditionally infectious diseases were thought to be non genetic. But host genetic factors play a major role in determining susceptibility to infection. To identify such factors, we have carried out genetic studies on patients with tuberculosis and their disease free household contacts. We will discuss our findings which are unique and/or validating previous results on other population.

Anshu Bharadwaj

IMTECH

Genome Analysis & Data Science

AI and structure driven approach for genome annotation. My lab is dedicated to unravelling the intricacies of complex phenotypes with a focus on infectious and mitochondrial diseases. I will provide glimpses of applications of 3Di alphabet in genome annotation for bacterial pathogens. I will also touch upon applications of data driven approaches in infection biology with specific focus on antimicrobial resistance.

Himanshu Sinha

IIT-Madras

Quantitative Genetics, Genomics, Transcriptomics

Activating the hidden backup circuit: Flip both switches simultaneously to change system behaviour. Two yeast variants individually activate separate metabolic pathways but together unlock a route to hidden arginine biosynthesis. Using comprehensive multi-omics analysis, we discovered this latent pathway activation enhanced sporulation efficiency and mitochondrial function. This ‘genetic switching’ mechanism reveals how variant combinations rewire cellular metabolism beyond simple additive effects, offering insights into complex trait architecture.

Jahnavi Joshi

CCMB

Evolutionary Biology

Evolutionary and ecological perspectives on venom evolution in predatory soil arthropods. We use proteo-transcriptomics to study venom evolution in scolopendrid centipedes, the oldest venomous terrestrial arthropods. Their venoms are dominated by low molecular-weight neurotoxins that evolved through multiple parallel additions and deletions across scolopendrid lineages. Venom proteins, particularly scoloptoxins, show strong positive selection. We also found a significant association between venom diversity and body size. We are generating data on other ecological predictors (e.g. diet) to understand the drivers of venom diversity and complexity, and will explore the role of venoms in scolopendrids diversification and speciation.

Aravind N A

ATREE

Phylogeography and Conservation Genomics

Clade age drove freshwater gastropod diversification. We asked whether species richness in freshwater gastropods (snails and slugs) from the Western Ghats was driven by clade age or diversification rates. Our findings indicate that older clades are more species-rich, while neither the shift to freshwater habitats nor the evolution of direct development significantly increased speciation rates. Miocene sea-level fluctuations and monsoon patterns led to the decline of diversification.

Shankar Manoharan

IIT-J

Microbial Pathogenesis

Armour less but not defenceless: Klebsiella pneumoniae adaptation to the neonatal bloodstream. Klebsiella pneumoniae (Kpn) causes of infant mortality in low resource settings. Investigation of a neonatal sepsis outbreak in Jodhpur identified a Kpn isolate carrying a capsule-inactivating insertion. As the Kpn capsule confers resistance to complement killing, permitting bloodstream survival, the finding was counterintuitive. Subsequent analyses showed that capsule inactivation is common in neonatal sepsis-causing Kpn isolates and that secondary mutations, which can compensate for capsule loss, may enable Kpn’s adaptation to the bloodstream.

Sucharita Dey

IIT-J

Protein Engineering & Evolution

Identifying the molecular determinants of proteins’ robustness to mutations. The size and structural compactness of proteins directly influence their evolvability. Both tiny and huge proteins are less evolvable. Properties underlying structural compactness include local atomic density, shape complementarity, contact density, and intra-subunit hydrogen bonds. The more compact the protein, the more evolvable and robust it is. We will use disease-causing versus neutral mutations to train a machine learning-based classifier to predict the deleterious effects of substitution mutations.

Sushmita Jha

IIT-J

Neuro-oncology & Immunology

Unlocking Glioma Mysteries: Biomarkers, patient-derived organoids, and tumour-immune dialogues. Malignant gliomas are highly lethal brain tumours. This talk explores how biomarker discovery and the generation of patient-derived organoids are revealing new dimensions of tumour diversity and microenvironment. By dissecting innate immune signalling and tumour-immune interactions, we uncover actionable pathways and provide platforms for targeted drug testing.

Murali Dharan Bashyam

CDFD

Molecular Oncology

‘I open at the close’: Gene Fusions activate genes from closed chromatin compartments across cancer types. I shall describe the complex relationship between chromatin compartments and gene fusions. We found that most fusions arise from genes located in the ‘A’ (open; gene rich, active histone marks, active transcription) rather than the ‘B’ (close; gene poor, repressive histone marks, suppressed transcription) chromatin compartment. More importantly, fusion of genes from orthogonal compartments (A-B fusion) activates the 3’ (B) partner gene driven by strong transcriptional signals of the 5’ (A) partner gene.

Madhusudan Reddy Nandineni

CDFD

Forensic Genetics

DNA profiling in criminal investigations and mass disaster victim identification. DNA profiling has been crucial in resolving cases of homicides, sexual assaults, burglaries, missing persons, and has aided in the identification of victims of natural disasters and mass casualty events, thus providing closure to families. My presentation will discuss landmark cases, alongside the methodologies used, and challenges in DNA collection and analysis.

Participating Non-Speaker PIs

Distinguished researchers participating in the symposium

Rohit Joshi

CDFD

Drosophila Developmental Neurogenetics

Gene and its tissue-specific enhancers show epigenetic synchrony during development. Robustness of tissue-specific gene expression of the conserved Drosophila transcription factor Grainyhead (Grh) in neural stem cells (NSCs) of the CNS relies on the cumulative activity of multiple enhancers. Different enhancer combinations regulate gene expression in different subsets of NSCs. Gene and its enhancers show epigenetic synchrony across the cell types of CNS. Transient expression of a fate determinant in an intermediate stem cell progenitor is due to protein inherited from the mother stem cell.

Kartiki V. Desai

NIMG Kalyani

Breast Cancer Genomics

Networks in Endocrine Therapy Resistance. While hormone receptor positive breast cancer has the best prognosis, the most common cause of recurrence is resistance to endocrine therapy. Since most genomic aberrations in this cancer result in quantitative changes in gene expression, our lab attempts to understand the biology of genetic regulators and RNA networks perturbed by them. Highlights of these data from endocrine resistant cancer cell models will be discussed.

Karthik Tallapaka

CCMB

Medical Genetics & Exome Sequencing

Metagenomic profiling of antimicrobial resistance in wastewater from Cities - a two-year longitudinal study. Wastewater samples collected from March 2022 to March 2024 from four Indian cities were profiled by shotgun metagenomics to monitor microbial diversity, antimicrobial resistance genes (ARGs) and mobile genetic elements (MGEs). Significant differences in microbial community were found with city specific clustering, suggesting distinct local environmental influences. ARGs conferring resistance to important antibiotics (eg tetracyclines and beta-lactams) showed higher association with MGEs in contrast to macrolide resistance genes.

Tavpritesh Sethi

IIIT-Delhi

Data Science & Generative AI

Advancing Gene Signature Discovery with Generative AI. My work developed a novel pipeline using generative AI–driven workflow for uncovering conserved, statistically significant gene signatures and pathways from small transcriptomic datasets. The performance was validated in six diseases including cancers and infections.

D P Kasbekar

CDFD (Retired)

DNA Fingerprinting

DNA profiles and the expertise to evaluate them. I found two DNA profiles (DNA fingerprints), generated by CDFD DNA technicians, that contained information the technicians missed noticing. Such information, obvious to geneticists (even retired ones), would also be missed by other non-experts (police, lawyers, judges). Questions raised by such overlooked information would remain unaddressed, and thus adversely impact delivery of justice. (Dialogue-Science, Scientists and Society vol 8, 2025).
